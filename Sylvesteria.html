Robin Hood Temporary Relief Program

    The Robin Hood Temporary Relief Program is funded by taxes and can give people who qualify on certain conditions up to $100,000. This is NOT A loan, and maximum income is $25,000. Maximum credit score is 650. The application will ask for name, account number (if any, then paid in cash), adress, annual income, unemployment time (in months) if any at all, mouths to feed (household size), credit score, and ID (if any at all to verify that you are not a rich person). It is then converted into a score from 1 to 100 that determines how much money is made. If amounts inputted are exceeding maximum limit, the application will be flagged and sent to the Secretary of the Treasury to investigate the matter. If fraud has been discovered, the case will be sent over to Parliament and the Supreme Court to sentence the person behind the fraudulent application as the law says.
